# Final second-pass decision: Pactwire

Research snapshot: 2026-07-19. This decision ignores schedule and implementation-time constraints. It applies the official equal-weight rubric and treats external truth, decision authority, prior art, and model ablation as score gates.

## Decision

**Select Pactwire—the district DPA behavior-regression tripwire—in the Education track as the sole evidence-adjusted hackathon bet. Confidence: medium.**

It is not a proven new category. It is a differentiated procurement-assurance wedge whose closest predecessors already perform behavior-versus-contract audits. It wins this tournament because it has the best combination of a consequential documented problem, a deep system, a complete user journey, and an externally witnessed state change that can be demonstrated without asking GPT-5.6 to certify its own work.

The precise proposition is:

> For a district privacy officer responsible for a signed DPA, replay authorized synthetic student and teacher journeys after a meaningful product change; if deterministic instrumentation witnesses a newly contradictory data flow or loses required observability, change the district's app state from `APPROVED` to `HOLD` pending human review. Never infer compliance and never restore approval automatically.

The **Completion Offer Network** is the stronger conditional category thesis, but not the stronger hackathon bet on the current evidence. It becomes selectable only after at least two receiving institutions agree to issue meaningfully scoped, pre-deposit, major-specific signed offers. A simulated registrar cannot create that outcome.

No surviving direction is yet a proven standalone category.

The offer network's indispensable output is another institution's obligation; without real issuers, its strongest demo beat is fictional rather than merely incomplete.

## Why the selected mechanism is not theater

The three-minute proof must use a controlled test application or an explicitly authorized partner tenant and show all of the following:

1. A human-confirmed DPA clause, named student/teacher journeys, and synthetic canary fields are fixed before the run.
2. GPT-5.6 operates an unfamiliar authenticated workflow; a separate deterministic layer captures requests, destinations, timestamps, field propagation, screenshots, and hashes.
3. A planted canary appears in a request that contradicts the reviewed test obligation. The product cites the packet and clause and says **witnessed contradiction**, not “illegal” or “noncompliant.”
4. A real approval record changes from `APPROVED` to `HOLD`, routing the receipt to the privacy officer.
5. After a fixed version is replayed, the result says only **not re-observed in these named journeys**. A human, not the model, decides whether approval returns.

GPT-5.6 is structural only if its journey discovery and contract-to-scenario proposals produce materially more contract-relevant coverage per expert hour than a human-recorded browser script plus the same proxy and deterministic rules. The model may explore, map, and repair journeys. It may not fabricate traffic, interpret law, determine compliance, hash evidence, or control the approval rule. If a deterministic recorder matches its coverage and authoring effort, the model-native claim fails and the concept loses its 4 in Technological Implementation.

The selected direction also has a hard safety asymmetry: a witnessed regression may automatically place an approval on hold; missing evidence may never create or restore approval. `NOT EXERCISED` and `NOT OBSERVABLE` must remain first-class states. The sandbox must contain no child data and must isolate the computer-use agent from district admin sessions, broad credentials, and prompt-injection-driven tool access.

## Why the stronger category thesis is not the selection

The [binding-transfer audit](binding-transfer-novelty.md) found one plausible new market object: multiple institutions issue comparable, signed statements of how a student's verified credits apply to a named degree before the enrollment deposit. That changes `institution estimate` into `institution obligation` and could reshape the transaction.

Authority is correctly placed in the product—the institution is an issuer, not an external oracle casually omitted from the design. Adoption is nevertheless existential rather than incidental. DegreeSight, Stellic, Transferology, Transfer Explorer, CollegeSource, CUNY tools, registrar evaluations, and bounded transfer guarantees already supply nearly every computational and workflow component. The residual is the institution's willingness to commit early, expose remaining requirements comparably, accept a remedy, and avoid blanket disclaimers. One institution creates an admissions feature; at least two create the proposed network.

Therefore:

- with two real issuers and offer-to-final-audit evidence, the Completion Offer Network becomes the best **standalone-category** candidate in this pool;
- with mock reviewers or unsigned estimates, it collapses into a polished transfer planner and its Design and Impact claims are theater;
- GPT-5.6 matters only if it reduces authorized review time or expands complex-case coverage against current AI-assisted degree-mapping products. It cannot award credit or sign the offer.

This is an authority and causal-validity judgment, not a schedule judgment.

## Why the other directions lose

- **Capability settlement:** real problem, clean state change, weak white space. Khanmigo-style next-item checks, LMS mastery states, delayed assessment, adaptive probes, and practical performance checks already occupy the mechanism. Select it only if the portable settlement object changes a consequential learner or educator decision and GPT-5.6 reduces expert probe work at equal validity.
- **Manufacturing witness:** the paired artifact is memorable, but the materiality oracle is outside the incomplete package. A signed CTQ, deterministic conformance proof, engineer adjudication, and incremental lift over F4/Siemens plus Autodesk/CETOL-style analysis are mandatory. Without them, the demo is attractive variation, not a witnessed defect.
- **Broad local ADE / executable forks:** Replit Agent 4, Figma, v0, Acepe, and pm7Code already own variants, parallel execution, previews, selection, and persistent preference primitives. The residual executable-clarification protocol must beat one optimized question and one cheap mockup on total owner attention. Until then it is an ADE feature with very high absorption risk.
- **Causal repository history:** Rex/ROSE, temporal-coupling tools, change contracts, delta debugging, mutation testing, and merge queues occupy the pieces. Its ablation-failure-rescue witness is interesting only if it catches held-out omissions beyond unchanged CI; otherwise it launders an existing test into a causal claim.
- **Dependency authority receipt:** provenance, source policy, package-health gates, and capability resolution already exist. Requiring a preexisting trust root prevents slopsquatting but risks collapsing into an evidence-rich allowlist. GPT-5.6 cannot solve the authority-bootstrap dilemma.
- **Agent-tool compatibility lock:** paired with/without evaluation is ordinary task-specific configuration evaluation, and current eval platforms can already run it. The honest output is an expiring non-regression receipt inside a gateway or eval product, not a package manager. Its audited ceiling is 10/16.
- **Worker cohort pay reconciliation:** the Shipt Calculator, FairFare, Worker Info Exchange, WageWatch, and privacy-threshold products compress novelty. Without worker-organization governance, a realistic re-identification threat model, and affected-worker evidence, the safety and outcome claims fail.

No Developer Tools/ADE concept in the reviewed set merits lead selection. Their implementation can be impressive, but the equal-weight rubric prevents technical depth from compensating for feature-level novelty and weak causal impact evidence.

## Kill conditions for the selected bet

Kill the tripwire as the lead if any one of these remains true after the decisive experiment:

1. GPT-5.6 does not materially beat a human-recorded deterministic replay baseline on contract-relevant path coverage per expert hour.
2. The system cannot reproduce a witnessed contradiction from captured external state with high precision and stable evidence hashes.
3. The approval state is only a dashboard label rather than a buyer-controlled operational state.
4. The product emits a general compliance conclusion, treats unobserved behavior as safe, or restores approval automatically.
5. Authorization, prompt-injection isolation, and synthetic-data boundaries cannot be demonstrated.

## Final call

Build **Pactwire**, the district DPA behavior-regression tripwire, as the Education-track hackathon bet. Do not call it an AI privacy scanner, an automated compliance gate, or a new category. Its winning thesis is narrower and stronger: **turn a district-specific contract regression into a replayable external witness and a reversible institutional hold.**

Keep the **Completion Offer Network** as the best conditional category bet. Promote it above the tripwire only when real institutional issuers make its central state change non-fictional.
