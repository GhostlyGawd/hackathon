# Independent market and substitutes review

Research snapshot: 2026-07-19. This review deliberately does **not** propose products or mechanisms. It asks a narrower question: after naming current substitutes and current policy movement, which failure events still look structurally under-served?

Product pages establish that a substitute exists and what it claims to do; they do not establish efficacy. Regulatory pages establish the current direction of travel, not that implementation has eliminated a failure. I did not use build time as a ranking factor.

## Bottom line

The atlas contains many real harms, but real harm is not the same thing as white space. The most crowded ideas are now health-insurance appeal generation, AI tutoring that withholds answers, AI-era oral assessment, caregiver coordination, bereavement administration, frontline microlearning, architecture guardrails, agent-security gateways, and general agent evaluation. Several would reproduce a live product's headline almost verbatim.

The strongest residual opportunities are narrower contradictions that the incumbent category avoids:

1. keeping open-source contribution open without making maintainer trust infinitely expensive;
2. predicting whether a *composition* of individually valid MCP tools will degrade a particular agent;
3. giving the weaker party an auditable account when the stronger party controls the records;
4. detecting cumulative or historical software-change obligations that no individual PR owns;
5. resolving consequential evidence after it has crossed institutional boundaries.

Even these are hypotheses. Several have excellent problem evidence but weak evidence that a standalone product can alter the outcome.

## Ranked top ten problem surfaces still under-served

### 1. Open-source maintainers cannot preserve openness and bound the cost of trust

**Why it remains under-served.** GitHub's response to low-quality volume is principally channel control: maintainers can limit who may open pull requests, while templates and repository policy set expectations. GitHub explicitly says review remains human-time intensive. An [Anti Slop GitHub Action](https://github.com/marketplace/actions/anti-slop) scores or blocks submissions, and [Copilot code review](https://docs.github.com/en/copilot/how-tos/use-copilot-agents/request-a-code-review/use-code-review) adds another per-PR reviewer. Those substitutes filter, label, or review an artifact. They do not cheaply establish that a newcomer understands the change, will respond coherently, and can maintain it. Blocking newcomers resolves the attention problem by discarding openness, which is one side of the contradiction rather than a resolution.

- **Named substitutes:** GitHub pull-request limits and contribution policy; repository templates; Anti Slop; Copilot code review; conventional CI and CODEOWNERS.
- **Fatal flaw:** Any “proof of understanding” can itself be generated by an agent. A new gate can become either a gameable classifier or another chore for the very maintainer it claims to protect. Some maintainers may rationally prefer closing unsolicited contribution channels, leaving no buyer demand for a subtler system.
- **Confidence:** **High** that the failure is current and under-served; **medium** that it supports a defensible product category.
- **What would change my mind:** Evidence that GitHub's native limits restore maintainer time and one-time-contributor merge rates without excluding valuable newcomers; or a GitHub-native mechanism that measures contribution ownership and follow-through with no extra maintainer work. In the other direction, outcome data showing that maintainers accept a new kind of newcomer evidence would raise confidence.

### 2. MCP capability composition has no accepted compatibility signal

**Why it remains under-served.** The official [MCP Inspector](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/docs/tools/inspector) checks connectivity, schemas, prompts, resources, and individual tool execution. The [official MCP Registry](https://registry.modelcontextprotocol.io/) standardizes discovery. [MCPBench](https://mcpbench.ai/) and academic benchmarks characterize servers or model tool use. None of these sources claims to answer the specific question in DT-04: whether adding server B to a working model/client/server-A configuration lowers end-to-end task performance because of naming, description, context, or behavioral interference. General eval platforms such as [Braintrust](https://www.braintrust.dev/docs/evaluate) and [LangSmith](https://www.langchain.com/langsmith/evaluation) can run a team-authored experiment, but they do not provide an ecosystem-level composition result.

- **Named substitutes:** MCP Inspector; official MCP Registry; MCPBench; MCP-Bench; manually curated tool subsets; Braintrust; LangSmith.
- **Fatal flaw:** The evidence base is essentially one very recent Microsoft Research line of work. “Tool-space interference” may be a transient property of current context strategies, and model or client-side dynamic tool routing may absorb it. Buyers may experience it as vague agent unreliability rather than a problem worth purchasing against.
- **Confidence:** **Medium.** This is the cleanest market white space in the atlas and one of the weakest validated pains.
- **What would change my mind:** Independent production traces showing material regressions when useful MCP tools are added; replication across GPT-5.6 and multiple clients; and evidence that developers currently spend money or substantial time isolating bad combinations. Conversely, robust client-side tool routing that makes performance invariant to the registered tool set would eliminate the opportunity.

### 3. Hourly workers cannot audit pay from records they do not control

**Why it remains under-served.** The Department of Labor's free [DOL-Timesheet app](https://blog.dol.gov/2023/01/26/dol-timesheet-app-now-available-in-spanish) tracks hours and calculates expected pay; DOL says worker-created records can help in a dispute. Emerging products such as [OverPay](https://socialreporthq.com/overpay) and [AuditPay](https://auditpay.co/) compare personally entered shifts with payslips. Payroll portals expose the employer's final record, and DOL's [PAID program](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/paid) lets willing employers self-audit. None gives the worker a shared provenance trail across schedule changes, time-clock edits, break deductions, rate rules, tips, and payroll output. Federal law requires the employer to keep records but does not itself require a pay stub, according to the [DOL FLSA Advisor](https://webapps.dol.gov/elaws/faq/esa/flsa/018.htm).

- **Named substitutes:** DOL-Timesheet; OverPay; AuditPay; employer payroll/time-clock portals; pay calculators; worker centers and wage-and-hour complaints; PAID.
- **Fatal flaw:** The exact consumer app category is already emerging, so a “better paycheck auditor” is a feature, not white space. More importantly, self-recorded evidence cannot reveal an employer's hidden edits or compel correction. Jurisdiction-specific wage rules, retaliation risk, cash work, and unauthorized-practice-of-law boundaries make the last mile harder than the arithmetic.
- **Confidence:** **High** on pain and substitute inadequacy; **medium** on remaining market space.
- **What would change my mind:** Widespread laws requiring workers to receive machine-readable time-edit and pay-calculation histories; payroll systems providing those histories by default; or outcome studies showing that existing audit apps materially shorten disputes and increase recovered pay. Evidence that workers will continuously maintain independent records would raise product confidence.

### 4. Renters discover and dispute screening errors only after the home is lost

**Why it remains under-served.** TransUnion has a [rental-screening dispute process](https://www.transunion.com/client-support/rental-screening-disputes), RentGrow has its own [consumer dispute flow](https://www.rentgrow.com/learn-now/), and the [FTC](https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/disputing-errors-your-tenant-background-check-report) and [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/tenant-background-checks/review-your-rental-background-check/) explain FCRA rights. These remedies are company-specific and generally begin after adverse action. They can correct a report, but they do not hold the unit, normalize data lineage across screening firms, or force a landlord to reconsider. The July 2026 [FTC RentGrow settlement](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/rentgrow-pay-225-million-settle-ftc-allegations-company-violated-fair-credit-reporting-act-ftc-act) confirms that duplicate and inaccurate records remain current even after years of enforcement.

- **Named substitutes:** CRA dispute portals; CFPB complaints; FTC/CFPB guidance; legal aid and consumer attorneys; reusable tenant-screening-report laws; SmartMove's renter-pushed report.
- **Fatal flaw:** Only the CRA or data furnisher can authoritatively correct its file, and the landlord can rent the unit while the dispute proceeds. A third party can easily collapse into legal intake plus document organization. It would also centralize unusually sensitive credit, criminal, eviction, and identity data.
- **Confidence:** **High** that the contradiction persists; **medium-high** that the substitute gap is meaningful.
- **What would change my mind:** Evidence that the 2024 CFPB accuracy opinion and 2026 FTC orders materially reduce complaint rates and dispute time; broad adoption and landlord acceptance of reusable reports; or universal pre-application access to the exact screening data and scoring criteria a landlord will use. Data showing fast correction reliably restores the same housing opportunity would raise confidence substantially.

### 5. Individually green changes can create cumulative repository-level risk

**Why it remains under-served.** [GitHub merge queues](https://docs.github.com/en/pull-requests/collaborating-with-pull-requests/incorporating-changes-from-a-pull-request/merging-a-pull-request-with-a-merge-queue) test a PR against the latest target and queued changes; [rulesets](https://docs.github.com/en/repositories/configuring-branches-and-merges-in-your-repository/managing-rulesets/available-rules-for-rulesets) require reviews, status checks, and ownership. Architecture products such as [ArchRails](https://www.archrails.io/) and [Mneme](https://mnemehq.com/) enforce declared constraints. GitHub's [Agent HQ mission control](https://github.blog/ai-and-ml/github-copilot/how-to-orchestrate-agents-using-mission-control/) coordinates agent tasks. These are useful substitutes, but each primarily validates present changes against tests or declared rules. They do not claim to measure the longitudinal interaction effect described in DT-02: complexity and integration friction that becomes a repository property after many locally acceptable changes.

- **Named substitutes:** merge queues; branch rulesets; CI; code ownership; Copilot review; Agent HQ; ArchRails; Mneme; refactoring and code-health tools.
- **Fatal flaw:** The core evidence is new, preprint-stage, and uses aggregate repository measures that may not predict a concrete failure. “Repository-level property” may be architecture and team-governance debt rather than an agent-specific technical problem. Any broad score risks becoming an unactionable code-health dashboard.
- **Confidence:** **Medium-high** on the failure hypothesis; **medium** on market white space.
- **What would change my mind:** Independent replication linking the measured friction to incidents, review time, or maintenance cost; evidence that agent-heavy repositories experience the effect after controlling for change volume; or an incumbent showing cumulative-interaction validation rather than PR-by-PR checks. A finding that merge queues and smaller PRs erase the effect would demote it.

### 6. Conceptual software changes depend on undocumented historical companion changes

**Why it remains under-served.** CI, static dependency graphs, search, CODEOWNERS, and impact-analysis tools find encoded relationships. ArchRails and Mneme enforce relationships someone has already declared. Microsoft's Rex study is notable precisely because it learned correlations from history and was deployed internally across hundreds of repositories; I found no current general product that makes reliable, cross-artifact historical change obligations its primary promise. This distinguishes DT-06 from generic architecture governance and generic code review.

- **Named substitutes:** code search and Git history; CI; CODEOWNERS; static change-impact analysis; checklists; ArchRails; Mneme; Microsoft's internal Rex system.
- **Fatal flaw:** Historical correlation is not obligation. A system can fossilize accidental coupling, obsolete migrations, or bad practice and create alert fatigue. The strongest deployment evidence comes from one very large Microsoft environment; small and medium repositories may not have enough repeated history to learn anything dependable. The capability is also a natural feature for a code host or code-intelligence incumbent.
- **Confidence:** **Medium-high** that the failure is real; **medium** that the addressable segment is broad.
- **What would change my mind:** Replication outside large monorepos; measured precision on previously unseen companion changes; developer evidence that the warnings prevent defects rather than merely prompting extra edits. A commercial code host shipping history-derived companion-change warnings with strong accuracy would turn the space into an incumbent feature.

### 7. Small businesses must reconstruct disaster evidence after access to it has been damaged

**Why it remains under-served.** The [MySBA portal](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/disaster-assistance) accepts applications and shows status. FEMA/CFPB publish an [Emergency Financial First Aid Kit and Digital Backpack](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/disasters-and-emergencies/get-prepared-before-disaster-emergency-strikes/). The NAIC offers a [home-inventory app](https://content.naic.org/consumer/home-inventory), and numerous products store pre-loss inventories. Those substitutes help prepare, store, or submit. They do not reconstruct a defensible pre-disaster business baseline across tax, bank, insurance, payroll, inventory, photographs, repairs, and government rules once connectivity, staff, premises, and records have been disrupted.

- **Named substitutes:** MySBA Loan Portal and recovery centers; FEMA app/Digital Backpack; Emergency Financial First Aid Kit; NAIC Home Inventory; insurer claim portals; accountants; home- and business-inventory apps.
- **Fatal flaw:** The tool is most valuable after the event but needs trusted data access before the event. Truly missing evidence cannot be inferred safely, while synthetic reconstruction would create fraud and audit risk. The atlas evidence does not isolate what share of SBA denials or withdrawals documentation could have prevented. Without a causal estimate, this may be a vivid but infrequent workflow whose root constraint is underwriting or liquidity.
- **Confidence:** **Medium** on opportunity, despite high confidence in the event itself.
- **What would change my mind:** SBA administrative data identifying documentation as a major preventable cause of delay, withdrawal, or denial; proof that common institutions expose enough recoverable evidence after a disaster; and observed demand for preparedness beyond checklists. Evidence that recovery centers or portal changes already resolve repeated-submission failures would demote it.

### 8. School app approval compares contracts with claims, not claims with live data behavior

**Why it remains under-served.** [1EdTech TrustEd Apps](https://www.1edtech.org/certification/data-privacy/faq) explicitly reviews public privacy policies and terms. [Lightspeed Insight](https://www.lightspeedsystems.com/products/lightspeed-digital-insight/) inventories app and URL usage, watches policy changes, and integrates 1EdTech/SDPC vetting. [Netskope for K-12](https://www.netskope.com/solutions/k12) can inspect encrypted traffic as a security platform. These products cover much of procurement, shadow IT, and network visibility. The remaining gap is a defensible comparison between app-specific observed collection/transmission/retention and the DPA or approved purpose over time. Utah's current [technical investigation](https://schools.utah.gov/studentdataprivacy/files/Utah%20EdTech%20App%20Data%20Collection%20and%20Sharing%20-%202023-25%20Investigation.pdf) found that schools cannot realistically supply the expertise and time themselves. The amended [COPPA rule](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/01/ftc-finalizes-changes-childrens-privacy-rule-limiting-companies-ability-monetize-kids-data) and a June 2026 [FTC edtech order](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/06/ftc-gives-final-approval-order-against-illuminate-settling-allegations-it-failed-secure-students) keep the accountability pressure current.

- **Named substitutes:** 1EdTech TrustEd Apps; Lightspeed Insight; LearnPlatform; SDPC agreements; District Secure; Netskope; manual legal/network review.
- **Fatal flaw:** This is perilously close to a feature of endpoint management, secure web gateways, or Lightspeed. Inspecting children's decrypted traffic can itself create the privacy and security risk being audited. A packet destination does not reveal lawful purpose, retention, downstream use, or contract interpretation; expert review remains necessary.
- **Confidence:** **Medium-high** on the verification gap; **medium-low** that it is standalone white space.
- **What would change my mind:** A current product demonstrating continuous payload-to-DPA verification, not just app discovery and policy review; district procurement evidence; or a regulatory standard for machine-verifiable data-use declarations. Conversely, proof that Lightspeed/Netskope already produce legally useful behavior-to-contract findings would make this only a feature.

### 9. Transfer students cannot obtain binding degree applicability before commitment

**Why it remains under-served.** [CollegeSource TES and Transferology](https://collegesource.com/transfer-tools/tes/) support course equivalency and routing to faculty. [Transfer Explorer](https://transferexplorer.org/content/faqs) can show credit applicability using data updated from member institutions. Its own FAQ is the market diagnosis: it is exploratory, not an official decision, and cannot account for every scenario or independently verify institutional data. Articulation tables and advisors provide the same kind of nonbinding guidance. None can grant the student the binding, major-specific answer needed before enrollment because the receiving institution owns that authority.

- **Named substitutes:** Transferology; CollegeSource TES; Transfer Explorer; state articulation portals; degree-audit tools; admissions and academic advisors; unofficial transcript evaluations.
- **Fatal flaw:** This may not be software white space at all. The missing object is an institutional commitment, not a better inference. Without receiving-school participation and liability, another service must carry the same disclaimer and risks giving false certainty. With participation, it becomes an institutional workflow/standards change with long procurement and governance dependencies.
- **Confidence:** **High** that the student failure remains; **low-medium** that an independent product can resolve it.
- **What would change my mind:** Institutions offering pre-admission, program-specific guarantees through an API or signed artifact; longitudinal evidence that an existing platform accurately predicts final degree applicability; or a portable national/state standard that binds receiving schools. Evidence that transfer loss is now mostly caused by student major changes rather than opaque evaluation would demote it.

### 10. Patients return home with medication lists but no authoritative reconciliation of conflicts

**Why it remains under-served.** [MyChart](https://www.mychart.org/l/en-us/features/view-medications-test-results-bills/) exposes medications and care plans. [Medisafe](https://medisafeapp.com/) imports or records medication lists, warns about interactions, reminds patients, and shares reports. Medicare covers [transitional care management](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/transitional-care-management-services), and pharmacists can perform medication therapy management. These substitutes work when there is a sufficiently authoritative regimen or a professional is available to reconcile it. They do not make the discharge list, old bottles, pharmacy fills, portal, and verbal instructions agree, nor can a consumer infer which prescriber intent supersedes which snapshot.

- **Named substitutes:** MyChart medication lists/Care Companion; Medisafe and other medication managers; discharge summaries; community pharmacists; medication therapy management; transitional-care calls.
- **Fatal flaw:** Only an authorized clinician or pharmacist can safely resolve many conflicts. A model that chooses an answer creates severe clinical risk; one that merely flags differences adds work at a moment of low capacity. Without signed order provenance and responsive clinical handoff, the information layer cannot close the loop and easily degrades into another medication list or reminder.
- **Confidence:** **High** on the failure; **medium** on substitute inadequacy; **low-medium** on an independent product's ability to change the outcome.
- **What would change my mind:** Health systems exposing signed medication-change provenance across settings; evidence that existing transition-care programs reach most high-risk discharges; or trials showing a non-clinician-facing reconciliation workflow reduces discrepancies without increasing unsafe self-management. A standardized authoritative medication plan would make much of this an EHR feature.

## Policy or incumbent movement that makes a direction stale or unstable

- **Medicaid procedural renewal:** CMS's [streamlining final rule](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/streamlining-medicaid-childrens-health-insurance-program-and-basic-health-program-application) requires ex-parte use of available data and a 90-day reconsideration period after procedural termination. At the same time, a June 2026 [community-engagement rule](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicaid-community-engagement-requirement-certain-individuals-interim-final-rule-comment-period-cms) adds new verification at application and renewal for some adults. The pain is not stale, but the rules are moving fast and state implementation determines the actual failure. A generic renewal assistant would be brittle policy glue.
- **Prior authorization:** [CoverMyMeds](https://www.covermymeds.health/) already operates an integrated authorization network, while the [CMS interoperability and prior-authorization rule](https://www.cms.gov/initiatives/burden-reduction/overview/interoperability/policies-regulations/cms-interoperability-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f) drives payer APIs and process changes, principally for 2027. The current burden is real; packet assembly is a crowded and regulation-sensitive direction.
- **IRS notices:** The IRS now provides a [Document Upload Tool](https://www.irs.gov/help/irs-document-upload-tool), puts most notices in [online accounts](https://www.irs.gov/payments/online-account-for-individuals-frequently-asked-questions), and shows audit dates and response deadlines. Notice comprehension remains imperfect, but a third-party “explain and upload” assistant is being squeezed between official digitization and professional tax representation.
- **Manufacturing design intent:** [PTC model-based definition](https://www.ptc.com/en/technologies/cad/model-based-definition) and [Siemens Designcenter MBD](https://www.siemens.com/en-gb/products/designcenter/cad-software/mcad/model-based-definition-mbd/) already promise authoritative 3D product/manufacturing information and CAD/CAM continuity. The remaining problem is standards adoption, supplier interoperability, and organizational process. A new translator is likely a CAD/PLM feature and can add another lossy representation.
- **Construction revision control, cloud incident triage, freight demurrage audit, and research disclosure forms:** the raw atlas already names mature platforms and current rule changes. These failures deserve work, but their obvious automation layer is already sold by incumbents or is being standardized by regulators.

## Alleged white space that is probably an incumbent feature

- A causal explanation of a PR or “developer mental model” is likely a code-host/IDE/repository-intelligence feature unless it preserves something current code and history cannot recover.
- API migration and consumer-contract inference are natural extensions of API gateways, observability, contract testing, and developer portals.
- A family-care authority model belongs partly in EHR identity, consent, and proxy-access infrastructure; a separate family app cannot make its assertions authoritative.
- A more legible report card or evidence-linked educational answer belongs in the LMS, assessment platform, search product, or source tool unless it changes how mastery is measured.
- An accessibility overlay or task-completion agent does not remove the public body's duty to remediate the underlying service and risks becoming a browser/assistive-technology feature.
- Scam detection, medication reminders, health-record aggregation, directory search, and letter explanation are mature feature sets. The raw atlas correctly warns against treating their unresolved institutional contradictions as permission to build another surface-level app.
- Agent security policy, architecture rules, and eval traces are rapidly consolidating into gateways, code hosts, and agent platforms. A thin standalone layer is especially vulnerable to absorption.

## Preserved dissent and research gaps

1. **Developer Tools dominates this ranking for novelty, not evidence maturity.** DT-02 and DT-04 may be artifacts of a rapidly changing 2026 tool stack. The Apps and Work problems generally have stronger harm evidence but more structural authority and integration barriers.
2. **Tenant screening and wage proof have unusually clear weaker-party stakes.** They also risk becoming legal-document organizers that cannot compel the stronger institution. Impact should not be confused with control over the outcome.
3. **Transfer credit is a near-perfect problem and a suspect product opportunity.** Better reasoning cannot substitute for a receiving institution's binding decision.
4. **Medication reconciliation is under-served because authority matters.** That same fact makes model autonomy unsafe. Flagging uncertainty without a responsive clinician may only move burden onto the patient.
5. **The market scan is not proof of nonexistence.** I found no accepted composition-compatibility product for MCP and no general commercial equivalent to Rex, but emerging and internal tools may be unindexed. Those two claims need practitioner interviews and procurement searches before finalist status.
6. **No ranking item has demonstrated willingness to pay.** The atlas establishes workarounds and harm, not buyer, budget, adoption channel, or outcome lift. Those are the most important next falsification questions.

## Independent recommendation to the tournament

Advance ranks 1–6 to mechanism review only if reviewers keep the fatal flaws attached. Advance ranks 7–10 as high-value adversarial controls: any proposed mechanism must show how it overcomes missing authority, data access, or institutional response rather than merely organizing artifacts. Do not advance any item in the saturation table in its obvious product shape.
