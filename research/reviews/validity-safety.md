# Independent validity and safety review

Review date: 2026-07-19.

This review tests the four raw problem atlases against evidence quality, causal tractability, validation access, and risk to the person already bearing the harm. It does not score novelty, implementation time, or proposed solutions.

“Survives” means only that the failure event is sufficiently real and bounded to justify another research round. It does **not** mean that software can safely fix it. Evidence grades apply to the exact causal claim below, not to the importance of the general topic.

## Independent ranking

The ordering favors: (1) evidence that measures the claimed failure rather than adjacent scale; (2) a causal boundary that can be stated without pretending an institution, scarce resource, or expert judgment is software; (3) a non-circular validation outcome; and (4) access to affected users and ground truth. Severity alone does not overcome weak causality or unsafe validation.

## 1. AI-assisted performance can conceal weaker independent learning

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** In the cited preregistered experiment with nearly 1,000 high-school mathematics students, unrestricted GPT-4 improved assisted practice performance but produced 17% lower unassisted exam performance than the no-AI condition, and students did not accurately perceive the decrement. This supports a risk in that intervention, subject, age group, and outcome window; it does not establish that AI assistance generally harms learning. [PNAS](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2422633122)
- **Why grade B:** One strong controlled primary study directly tests the claimed mechanism, with national usage data corroborating reach but not harm. It is not yet replicated across subjects, ages, assistance styles, retention intervals, or accessibility needs.
- **Fatal flaw:** Any evaluation that measures success while assistance is present, or asks the same model to grade its own help, recreates the illusion. Hidden monitoring or restricting assistance can also become surveillance, punish legitimate accessibility use, and widen differences in available human support.
- **Validation requirement:** A preregistered comparison using independently scored, unassisted near-transfer and far-transfer tasks plus delayed retention; explicit measurement of confidence calibration; accessibility and subgroup analysis; and informed consent. Work with minors requires appropriate institutional review and school/guardian safeguards. Practice completion and user satisfaction are invalid primary outcomes.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High for the reported setting, medium for generalization. Independent replications showing no unassisted decrement—or showing durable gains under materially different help while controlling for time-on-task and selection—would change the ranking.

## 2. Reviewing agent work can require reconstructing the work

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Experienced developers report distinct planning, monitoring, and post-hoc verification work when delegating to coding agents, and larger AI-enabled change batches are associated with slower review and lower stability. The evidence supports an oversight burden; it does not prove that agent-authored code is generally worse or that any review artifact creates justified confidence. [human-oversight study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05391), [Microsoft developer study](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/to-copilot-and-beyond-22-ai-systems-developers-want-built/), [DORA](https://dora.dev/ai/gen-ai-report/)
- **Why grade A:** Independent qualitative, large-sample mixed-methods, and delivery-research sources converge on the same practitioner-observed failure.
- **Fatal flaw:** There is no general correctness oracle. More explanations, claims, traces, or model-authored review can increase the reviewer’s reading burden while creating false confidence; self-review by the originating model is circular evidence.
- **Validation requirement:** Blinded, within-subject review of real repository changes with independently seeded or adjudicated defects. Measure correct accept/reject decisions, dangerous false acceptance, time, workload, and ability to identify unexamined claims against a diff-plus-tests baseline. The ground truth must not be produced by the agent under evaluation.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High that the burden exists, medium that it is reducible without moving work elsewhere. Evidence that experienced reviewers already make equally fast and reliable decisions from existing diffs/tests, or that added evidence reliably increases false confidence, would change the ranking.

## 3. Transfer-credit truth arrives after commitment

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Transfer students lose substantial credit, and course acceptance does not necessarily establish major-level applicability; qualitative work reports that students often receive authoritative outcomes late because public-facing transfer information is disconnected from internal evaluation. The evidence does not establish that information failure causes every lost credit, extra semester, or departure. [GAO](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-17-574), [AIR](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED663739.pdf), [Educational Researcher](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.3102/0013189X251410173)
- **Why grade A:** A national administrative analysis, direct interviews, and newer major-level research triangulate the same course-versus-degree and timing failure. The national magnitude is dated, so current sizing remains less certain than the mechanism.
- **Fatal flaw:** Only the receiving institution can issue an authoritative evaluation, and rules, catalog years, substitutions, residency requirements, and exceptions change. An unofficial answer presented as certainty could induce a student to accept debt or reject a better option; institutional incentives may also favor late commitment.
- **Validation requirement:** Historical and prospective comparisons against registrar-issued, major-specific evaluations, including exceptions and catalog-year changes. Report requirement-level precision/recall, consequential false assurance, update latency, disagreement reasons, and whether students understand that an estimate is non-authoritative. Validation requires registrar/advisor participation and student decision research, not catalog matching alone.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High on the mechanism, medium on present national reach. Broad pre-enrollment authoritative evaluation or current evidence showing minimal decision-relevant error would lower it; prospective evidence connecting earlier accurate information to changed enrollment, cost, or time would raise it.

## 4. Patients must contest health-insurance denials before care is lost

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Insurance-administration problems are common among insured adults and are associated with delayed/foregone care and financial harm. In one current Medicare Advantage skilled-nursing review, few denials were appealed and most appealed denials were overturned. The 95% figure cannot be generalized to all plans, services, or denials, and an overturned decision is not by itself proof that every initial denial was unlawful. [KFF](https://www.kff.org/affordable-care-act/kff-survey-of-consumer-experiences-with-health-insurance/), [HHS OIG](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2026/medicare-advantage-organizations-overturned-nearly-all-appealed-prior-authorization-denials-for-skilled-nursing-facility-admission-raising-concerns-about-initial-denials/)
- **Why grade A:** Representative consumer data and administrative file review independently establish prevalence, harm, low appeal, and reversibility in a defined setting.
- **Fatal flaw:** The decisive clinical record, coding context, plan criteria, and procedural state may be missing or controlled by the provider and insurer. A mistaken coverage, deadline, or medical-necessity statement can delay care or waive rights; processing power and opaque criteria are structural causes, not merely document disorder.
- **Validation requirement:** Deidentified closed cases with expert-labeled facts and outcomes, followed by prospective review with patient advocates, clinicians, and benefits counsel. Measure citation-level factual accuracy, deadline/route accuracy, material omissions, calibrated abstention, patient comprehension, privacy/security, and actual resolution without using appeal success alone as the causal endpoint. Recruitment must include patients with illness, disability, language, and digital-access barriers.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High that the event is severe and recurrent, medium that a bounded information intervention changes outcomes. Evidence that most failures turn on non-discoverable insurer discretion, clinical scarcity, or unavailable records would lower it; prospective evidence showing fewer abandoned valid appeals without more harmful delay would raise it.

## 5. AI contribution volume consumes open-source maintainer attention

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Maintainers incur human review cost before the quality of plausible submissions is known, and repositories/platforms have adopted contribution limits in response to growing low-quality volume. A recent mixed-methods study associates the AI-contribution wave with an estimated decline in one-time-contributor merge rates. The evidence does not justify treating AI use, newcomer status, writing style, or low prior activity as evidence of low quality. [AI-DDoS study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04003), [GitHub PR-limit rationale](https://github.blog/open-source/maintainers/how-pull-request-limits-are-cutting-down-the-noise/)
- **Why grade B:** The evidence is large and behaviorally grounded, with direct practitioner corroboration, but the principal causal estimate is recent preprint work and the counterfactual is difficult.
- **Fatal flaw:** Any proxy for “slop” can silently become contributor surveillance or a gate that disproportionately excludes legitimate newcomers, non-native English writers, disabled contributors, and unconventional but valuable changes. Reducing maintainer attention by exporting proof work to contributors can preserve the same total burden rather than reduce it.
- **Validation requirement:** Prospective, consented evaluation with multiple projects and maintainers using independently adjudicated contribution outcomes. Measure maintainer minutes, valuable-contribution recall, newcomer false rejection, appeal/recovery, mentoring burden, and subgroup disparities. Authorship inference and style detection must not be treated as ground truth.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** Medium-high. Replication across ecosystems and transparent causal methods would raise confidence; evidence that platform rate limits remove the burden without suppressing valuable newcomers, or that the measured decline is explained by repo selection and contributor mix, would lower it.

## 6. AI-generated dependencies create package-identity ambiguity

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** In the cited evaluation across 16 code-generating models, nonexistent package recommendations were persistent, and repeated plausible names create an attack surface if an adversary later registers them. The reported rates belong to those models, prompts, languages, and dates and cannot be projected to GPT-5.6 or current agent configurations. [USENIX Security 2025](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity25/presentation/spracklen), [OpenSSF guidance](https://best.openssf.org/Security-Focused-Guide-for-AI-Code-Assistant-Instructions)
- **Why grade B:** One peer-reviewed, broad primary study directly measures the event, with authoritative ecosystem guidance corroborating the threat. Current independent replication is still needed because model behavior changes quickly.
- **Fatal flaw:** Registry existence, popularity, provenance metadata, and even prior legitimacy cannot establish that a dependency is the intended or safe one at install time. A safety claim can therefore become a new confused-deputy path or false assurance.
- **Validation requirement:** Repeated, version-pinned evaluation across current models, prompts, ecosystems, languages, and agentic install behavior; immutable ground truth for intended packages; separate measurement of nonexistent names, typo/confusion, malicious registration, and compromised legitimate packages; and false-block rates on uncommon legitimate dependencies. Rates must be date-stamped.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High that the phenomenon exists, medium on present exposure. A sustained near-zero rate across current agent models and real repositories would lower it; observed install behavior or malicious registrations traceable to repeated recommendations would raise urgency.

## 7. A polished submission no longer establishes mastery

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** In one blind university field audit, injected GPT-4-written submissions were usually undetected and often scored above real submissions. This demonstrates that a polished take-home artifact can fail as evidence of authorship or mastery. It does not estimate how much real student work is AI-generated, nor does it show that a particular student lacks mastery. [PLOS One](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0305354)
- **Why grade B:** A strong field audit measures the assessment failure directly, and national teen reports corroborate that AI use/cheating is salient. Prevalence and cross-discipline replication remain incomplete.
- **Fatal flaw:** Inferring mastery or authorship from writing style, edit behavior, prompts, or interaction telemetry becomes an AI detector under another name. It invites surveillance and discriminatory discipline, especially for multilingual and disabled learners. Mastery requires independent evidence of performance, not a confidence score attached to an artifact.
- **Validation requirement:** Educator- and learner-designed studies tied to explicit learning objectives, with independent oral, practical, or unassisted transfer performance as the criterion. Measure false mastery and false non-mastery judgments, accessibility, subgroup fairness, teacher workload, contestability, and whether any signal is misused for discipline. Model authorship labels are not ground truth.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High that artifact quality and mastery can diverge, medium on the magnitude across education. Reliable evidence that existing assessment redesigns already produce scalable, non-surveillant mastery evidence would reduce opportunity; replication across disciplines would raise confidence.

## 8. Hourly workers can see incorrect pay but cannot verify the calculation

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Wage-payment failures affect many workers and generate large recoveries, while official schedule, clock-edit, rate, and payroll records are typically employer-controlled. In at least some disputes, the worker must reconstruct a calculation from partial personal records. The recovery totals do not estimate prevalence, prove employer intent, or show that evidence asymmetry caused every violation. [DOL enforcement data](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/data/charts/fair-labor-standards-act), [DOL recordkeeping](https://www.dol.gov/general/topic/wages/wagesrecordkeeping)
- **Why grade B:** Administrative outcomes establish consequential failures and regulation corroborates who controls required records. Direct prevalence and causal measurement of the reconstruction burden are still limited.
- **Fatal flaw:** The affected worker may not possess authoritative clock edits, break records, rate history, jurisdiction-specific rules, or proof of employer control. A mistaken accusation or mishandled record can expose a worker to retaliation, immigration/employment risk, or loss of trust; a numerical mismatch is not automatically unlawful wage theft.
- **Validation requirement:** Consented cases with worker advocates, payroll professionals, and adjudicated outcomes across jurisdictions and pay structures. Measure reconstruction accuracy, material omissions, false allegations, correction time, retaliation/privacy safeguards, and whether workers can contest employer records. Synthetic pay examples alone cannot validate the power and access failure.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** Medium-high on the event, medium on preventable share. Representative worker-dispute research would raise confidence; evidence that most recoveries arise from policy violations that no additional evidence could clarify would lower the information-framing rank.

## 9. Cross-cutting code changes depend on undocumented companion changes

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** In Microsoft’s reported environment, missed correlated changes were sufficiently recurrent for a deployed system to affect 4,926 changes across 360 repositories, while earlier practitioner research documented difficulty judging completeness and cross-component risk. Historical co-change is evidence of an implicit practice, not proof that the same companion edit is currently required. [Rex](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/rex-preventing-bugs-and-misconfiguration-in-large-services-using-correlated-change-analysis/), [change-understanding study](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/how-do-software-engineers-understand-code-changes-an-exploratory-study-in-industry/)
- **Why grade B:** A longitudinal field deployment plus independent practitioner research strongly supports the mechanism, but the evidence is concentrated in one large-company context.
- **Fatal flaw:** Learned correlation can encode obsolete architecture, cargo-cult practice, temporary migration work, or a common cause rather than a requirement. Treating correlation as obligation produces alert fatigue and can prevent intentional architectural change.
- **Validation requirement:** Time-split, repository-held-out evaluation with domain-expert labels and prospective follow-up. Measure consequential omissions caught, false requirements, alert burden, rule decay after migrations, and missed novel dependencies. A passing build and historical co-occurrence are not sufficient ground truth.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High in the reported setting, medium across ordinary repositories. Multi-organization replication would raise it; high rates of obsolete/noncausal correlations or negligible downstream defects would lower it.

## 10. Renters must contest hidden tenant-screening errors after a housing decision

- **Precise causal claim we can safely make:** Federal complaint analyses document many repeated allegations of wrong-person, outdated, and uncorrected tenant-screening records, often alongside missing adverse-action notice. Complaint counts establish a substantial recurring failure mode but have no population denominator and are not all adjudicated errors. They cannot support a claim that most screening reports or housing denials are inaccurate. [GAO](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107196), [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/archive/newsroom/cfpb-reports-highlight-problems-with-tenant-background-checks/)
- **Why grade B:** Two authoritative federal analyses corroborate the mechanism and scale of complaints. Verified error prevalence and causal housing outcomes remain undermeasured.
- **Fatal flaw:** The renter may never receive the report, data lineage, required notice, or time for correction, and neither a screening company nor landlord must treat a non-authoritative reconstruction as dispositive. Mishandling criminal, credit, identity, or eviction data can compound discrimination and privacy harm.
- **Validation requirement:** Legal-aid and fair-housing partnerships using consented, adjudicated dispute files; verification against source court/credit records; deadline and rights accuracy; correction/reconsideration outcomes; false identity links; and disparate-impact analysis. Complaint text or synthetic reports alone cannot validate institutional acceptance or harm reduction.
- **Confidence and what changes it:** High that the failure recurs, medium on prevalence and addressable share. A denominator study with verified error rates and outcomes would raise confidence; evidence that lack of housing supply or landlord discretion dominates despite corrected reports would lower it.

## Cross-cutting safety conclusions

1. **Do not confuse evidence assembly with authority.** Transfer credit, coverage, medication regimens, payroll legality, tenant records, and clinical plans all have an authoritative institution or expert. Reconstructing evidence does not confer decision rights.
2. **Do not use model agreement as ground truth.** A model that produces a claim cannot validate the same claim. Independent outcomes are available for learning transfer, seeded code defects, adjudicated disputes, and official evaluations; where they are unavailable, uncertainty must remain explicit.
3. **The most harmed actor may be the least reachable user.** Illness, disability, grief, limited English, digital exclusion, employment precarity, and disaster all reduce capacity to gather data or operate another tool. Validation only with technically fluent stand-ins would be invalid.
4. **A confident false negative can be worse than no answer.** This is especially true for medication changes, scams, legal evidence, insurance deadlines, safety training, and production incidents.
5. **Information cannot substitute for capacity or power.** Provider scarcity, institutional discretion, housing supply, maintenance funding, social-service capacity, and retaliation are structural. Any future causal claim must isolate the share changed by information before claiming impact.
6. **Surveillance is not evidence of understanding.** Authorship detection, edit telemetry, contributor profiling, attendance-risk scoring, and hidden learner monitoring create fairness and privacy harms without establishing mastery, intent, or quality.

## Bottom line

The safest high-confidence research surfaces are the ones with an external outcome and a bounded information failure: unassisted learning after assistance, correct review decisions on known code changes, official transfer evaluations, adjudicated pay/screening disputes, and time-split correlated-change outcomes. High-severity health, benefits, legal, and safety surfaces remain important, but they should not advance merely because their documents make an impressive demo. Without affected-user access, authoritative ground truth, expert review, and measured abstention, they are more likely to produce false assurance than demonstrated impact.
