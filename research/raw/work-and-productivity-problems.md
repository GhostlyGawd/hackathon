## Work & Productivity problem atlas

Evidence key: **[P]** prevalence/scale evidence; **[Q]** institutional or qualitative evidence. Scores are directional 1–5, not market sizing.

These are problem surfaces, not product recommendations. Their failures are unusually visible in a demo: misunderstood safety instruction, evidence missing after a disaster, shifts that do not reconcile to pay, a model/drawing conflict producing the wrong part, or the same research fact represented inconsistently across forms.

### Twelve strongest problem cards

#### 1. Frontline safety instructions are delivered, but understanding is not established

- **Actor / trigger:** A safety manager or foreman introduces a new task, machine, hazard, or crew to multilingual or low-literacy frontline workers.
- **Desired outcome:** Each worker understands the controls in language and vocabulary they use and can apply them onsite.
- **Current workaround:** Translated handouts, bilingual coworkers, interpreters, demonstrations, and attendance sheets.
- **Cost/harm:** NIOSH found language barriers were the most common challenge reported by seafood-processing safety managers, including workplaces with several languages where text alone was insufficient. OSHA requires instruction in language and vocabulary workers can understand and expects employers to verify understanding. [NIOSH, Jan. 2024](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/maritime/about/language-barriers-in-seafood-processing.html) [OSHA policy](https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/standardinterpretations/2010-04-28) **[Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Literal translation does not resolve site jargon, literacy, cultural interpretation, or whether a worker can apply the instruction. Completion records proxy comprehension.
- **Contradiction:** People need demonstrable comprehension, but current systems force organizations to record document delivery or attendance.
- **Confidence:** High for multilingual high-risk sectors; medium for nationwide causal prevalence. BLS recorded 1,229 Hispanic or Latino worker fatalities in 2024, but that figure must not be attributed to language barriers. [BLS 2024 CFOI](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cfoi.nr0.htm) **[P]**

#### 2. Small businesses must prove disaster losses when the disaster has disrupted their evidence

- **Actor / trigger:** A small-business owner applies for SBA physical or economic-injury assistance after a declared disaster.
- **Desired outcome:** Establish pre-disaster baseline, damage, eligibility, and unmet need quickly enough for the business to survive.
- **Current workaround:** Reconstruct tax, banking, insurance, financial, photographic, and repair evidence; resubmit records; make calls; await inspection.
- **Cost/harm:** GAO reviewed 578,122 SBA disaster-loan applications from FY2018–22; among applications accepted as meeting minimum requirements, roughly 40% were approved, 40% declined, and 18.5% withdrawn. Those outcomes cannot all be attributed to documentation. Applicants after the 2017 hurricanes nevertheless reported repeated document submission, staff turnover, and translation problems. [GAO-24-106682](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-106682) [GAO-20-168](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-20-168) **[P/Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Precise historical evidence is distributed across institutions and formats, while connectivity, premises, staff, and record access may all be impaired.
- **Contradiction:** People need defensible evidence precisely when the triggering event has damaged their access to that evidence.
- **Confidence:** High for scale and recurring process failure; medium on documentation’s preventable share. A February 2026 OIG file review found weak supporting evidence in a small sample of approved cases, but this is control evidence, not applicant-prevalence evidence. [SBA OIG 26-05](https://www.sba.gov/document/report-26-05-sbas-desktop-loss-verification-process) **[Q]**

#### 3. Hourly workers can see incorrect pay but cannot see or prove the calculation

- **Actor / trigger:** An hourly worker or payroll administrator finds that a paycheck does not match shifts, overtime, rates, or deductions.
- **Desired outcome:** Reconstruct the calculation, identify the discrepancy, and correct it promptly.
- **Current workaround:** Preserve schedule and time-clock screenshots, texts, personal timesheets, and pay stubs; escalate internally or file a complaint.
- **Cost/harm:** DOL recovered $184.4 million in FLSA back wages for 145,798 employees in FY2025. Enforcement recoveries are a lower bound, not prevalence. [DOL FY2025 FLSA data](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/data/charts/fair-labor-standards-act) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Schedule, timekeeping, pay rules, edits, and payroll output live separately; employers control the official records while workers mainly see the final result. DOL recordkeeping rules and its worker timesheet app implicitly acknowledge that asymmetry. [DOL recordkeeping](https://www.dol.gov/general/topic/wages/wagesrecordkeeping) **[Q]**
- **Contradiction:** People need a shared, auditable pay calculation, but current systems force the weaker party to reconstruct evidence controlled by the stronger party.
- **Confidence:** High. Generic time tracking itself is saturated; the unresolved event is proof and provenance after disagreement.

#### 4. Machinists receive lossy representations of engineering intent

- **Actor / trigger:** A machinist or manufacturing engineer begins a job whose model or drawing lacks tolerances, material/process information, or consistent CAD/CAM interpretation.
- **Desired outcome:** Know the exact manufacturable intent before material is cut.
- **Current workaround:** Stop work, interrupt engineering, recreate or annotate drawings, and manually convert files.
- **Cost/harm:** NIST modeled $8.4 billion spent by engineers answering questions or producing extra documentation and $3.8 billion by machinists doing related work. It estimated interoperability costs of $20.9–42.9 billion. These are modeled national estimates, not audited losses. [NIST, Feb. 2020; updated Feb. 2025](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2020/02/inadequate-modeling-data-costs-billions-us-manufacturers) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Product-manufacturing information is not consistently represented or adopted in 3D workflows; format translation and human convention lose meaning, with ambiguity discovered downstream.
- **Contradiction:** People need executable, unambiguous design intent, but current systems force humans to reinterpret lossy artifacts.
- **Confidence:** High for the failure mechanism; medium for the exact national-dollar estimates.

#### 5. Researchers repeatedly translate the same facts into incompatible oversight schemas

- **Actor / trigger:** A principal investigator or research administrator applies for, accepts, or reports on a federal award.
- **Desired outcome:** Satisfy accountability requirements once while preserving time for research.
- **Current workaround:** Re-enter equivalent information across agency, institutional, disclosure, and reporting forms, portals, and spreadsheets.
- **Cost/harm:** A 6,081-person FDP survey reported researchers spending 42% of federally funded project time on administration, although this is an older self-reported estimate. GAO later confirmed cumulative, agency-specific requirements and systems. [FDP survey](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2887040/) [GAO-16-573](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-16-573) **[P/Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Requirements differ by agency and office, change over time, and encode the same underlying facts differently.
- **Contradiction:** People need accountable oversight, but current systems force repeated translation of the same research facts into incompatible schemas.
- **Confidence:** Medium. GAO reported in 2026 that common disclosure forms have reduced some burden, so this is not untouched whitespace. [GAO-26-107544](https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-26-107544/index.html)

#### 6. Ocean-freight invoice disputes require reconstructing causality across adversarial records

- **Actor / trigger:** An importer, freight forwarder, or AP operator receives a detention or demurrage invoice following terminal closure, unavailable appointments, or delayed equipment return.
- **Desired outcome:** Establish whether the charge, amount, and billed party are valid before the deadline.
- **Current workaround:** Reconcile bills of lading, tariffs, terminal availability, gate events, appointment timestamps, emails, and screenshots.
- **Cost/harm:** Nine carriers reported collecting approximately $15.4 billion in detention and demurrage charges from April 2020 through March 2025; this does **not** mean those charges were improper. [FMC aggregate data](https://www.fmc.gov/detention-and-demurrage/) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Carrier, terminal, drayage provider, and customer each hold incomplete and sometimes self-interested portions of the timeline. FMC complaint guidance explicitly requests invoices, proof, screenshots, gate closures, and emails. [FMC complaint guidance](https://www.fmc.gov/ocean-shipping-reform-act-of-2022-implementation/guidance-on-charge-complaint-interim-procedure/) **[Q]**
- **Contradiction:** People need one causal event record, but current systems force them to prove it from parties with partial logs.
- **Confidence:** High. The 2024 FMC billing rule validates the problem but the space is now crowded. [FMC final rule](https://www.fmc.gov/articles/fmc-publishes-final-rule-on-detention-and-demurrage-billing-practices/)

#### 7. Legal teams cannot safely infer “nothing relevant” from incomplete multimodal review

- **Actor / trigger:** A public defender or prosecutor receives large volumes of body-camera footage, jail calls, interviews, and phone evidence before a hearing or trial.
- **Desired outcome:** Find every relevant, exculpatory, or inconsistent moment while preserving provenance and chain of custody.
- **Current workaround:** Serial viewing/listening, filenames, spreadsheets, handwritten notes, interns, transcription, and keyword search.
- **Cost/harm:** RAND found constitutionally appropriate public-defense work requires 13.5–286 hours depending on case type, forcing overloaded defenders to triage. NIJ found defense attorneys were especially behind in handling digital evidence and identified prioritization and triage as top needs. [RAND, 2023](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA2559-1.html) [NIJ, 2015](https://nij.ojp.gov/library/publications/digital-evidence-and-us-criminal-justice-system-identifying-technology-and) **[P/Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Evidence is heterogeneous, metadata and codecs vary, and legal duties make false negatives materially different from ordinary search misses.
- **Contradiction:** People need exhaustive review, but current systems force scarce humans to inspect evidence serially.
- **Confidence:** High for general workload; medium for the incremental national burden of digital evidence. NIJ funded a 2024 study precisely because body-camera workload effects remain incompletely quantified. [NIJ award](https://nij.ojp.gov/funding/awards/15pnij-24-gg-01793-ress)
- **Saturation:** JusticeText and Reduct already cover obvious transcription/search shapes. [JusticeText](https://www.justicetext.com/) [Berkeley Law tool catalog](https://www.law.berkeley.edu/research/criminal-law-and-justice-center/our-work/ai-for-public-defenders/existing-ai-tools/)

#### 8. Cloud incidents are routed through organizational ownership before causality is known

- **Actor / trigger:** An on-call engineer receives a high-severity alert assigned to the wrong team or lacking current dependency context.
- **Desired outcome:** Identify ownership, root cause, and a safe mitigation quickly.
- **Current workaround:** Reassign or page teams, inspect telemetry and chats, consult troubleshooting documents, and manually track hypotheses.
- **Cost/harm:** A Microsoft study of 20 online systems found 4.11–91.58% of incidents were reassigned at least once, depending on system; reassignment increased triage time by as much as 10.16×. [Microsoft/ICSE, 2019](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/an-empirical-investigation-of-incident-triage-for-online-service-systems/) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Dependency maps and health assessments are incomplete or stale, while evidence accumulates across telemetry, chat, documentation, permissions, and organizational boundaries. [Microsoft/FSE, 2020](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/?p=691560) [Microsoft/SoCC, 2022](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/uploads/prod/2022/09/3542929.3563482.pdf)
- **Contradiction:** People need one current causal picture, but systems force diagnosis from disconnected and stale signals.
- **Confidence:** High for large cloud systems.
- **Saturation:** PagerDuty and similar incident platforms already market AI-supported triage and context. [PagerDuty](https://www.pagerduty.com/platform/incident-management/incident-response/)

#### 9. Facility-maintenance prioritization is performed from stale, incomparable asset evidence

- **Actor / trigger:** A public-facilities manager must triage aging assets and a deferred-maintenance backlog under a constrained budget.
- **Desired outcome:** Sequence work by safety, mission, and cascade risk before failure.
- **Current workaround:** Inspections, asset databases, spreadsheets, contractor assessments, and expert judgment.
- **Cost/harm:** GAO reported a $137 billion DOD deferred-maintenance backlog for FY2020 with incomplete or inconsistent condition data, and a civilian-agency backlog rising from $51 billion in FY2017 to $76 billion in FY2021. [GAO-22-104481](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-22-104481) [GAO-23-106124](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-23-106124) **[P/Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Asset ages, condition scores, mission effects, and interdependencies are stale or incomparable across systems.
- **Contradiction:** People need prevention prioritized by risk, but current systems force decisions from weak evidence after resources are already insufficient.
- **Confidence:** High for the problem; low-to-medium for model leverage. Funding and staffing—not information alone—are major root causes. [GAO-26-106832](https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-26-106832/index.html)

#### 10. Construction crews must reconcile authoritative intent across organizations and revisions

- **Actor / trigger:** A superintendent or subcontractor is about to install work while drawings, RFIs, submittals, and change records disagree.
- **Desired outcome:** Know the current approved instruction and its downstream consequences.
- **Current workaround:** Compare records across Procore, ACC, Bluebeam, email, calls, overlays, and paper sets.
- **Cost/harm:** A vendor-funded Autodesk/FMI survey of 3,900 respondents reported that 30% considered more than half their project data “bad” and modeled $88.69 billion in rework attributable to bad data. Treat the estimate cautiously. [Autodesk/FMI, 2021](https://adsknews.autodesk.com/en/pressrelease/study-from-autodesk-and-fmi-finds-better-data-strategies-could-save-the-global-construction-industry-1-85-trillion/) A 2025 peer-reviewed Turkey study of 209 respondents also found management/planning and design/time constraints among major rework factors, with limited external validity. [Buildings, 2025](https://www.mdpi.com/2075-5309/15/4/606) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Artifacts and approval states cross organizational systems, offline copies persist, and version control does not ensure semantic consequences are reconciled.
- **Contradiction:** People need one authoritative, site-specific instruction, but current systems force field crews to reconcile lifecycle artifacts across organizations.
- **Confidence:** High for the mechanism; medium for scale.
- **Saturation:** Procore already markets current-drawing and obsolete-revision control. [Procore](https://www.procore.com/en-ca/project-management/drawings)

#### 11. Prior authorization asks clinicians to re-prove evidence already present in the chart

- **Actor / trigger:** A clinic prior-authorization specialist or physician encounters a payer requirement or documentation denial.
- **Desired outcome:** Establish coverage and medical necessity without delaying or abandoning treatment.
- **Current workaround:** Extract chart fragments, use portals/faxes/calls, appeal, and request peer review.
- **Cost/harm:** HHS OIG found that 13% of sampled denied Medicare Advantage prior-authorization requests met Medicare coverage rules; in some “insufficient documentation” cases, the existing record was sufficient. [HHS OIG, 2022](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2022/some-medicare-advantage-organization-denials-of-prior-authorization-requests-raise-concerns-about-beneficiary-access-to-medically-necessary-care/) **[P]**
- **Why tools fail:** Payer-specific criteria are opaque and changing, and patient evidence already stored in the chart is not consistently recognized or mapped.
- **Contradiction:** People need patient evidence applied once, but current systems force bespoke packets against opaque criteria.
- **Confidence:** High for burden and error.
- **Saturation/change risk:** CoverMyMeds reports a large integrated network, and CMS interoperability/API requirements are scheduled principally for 2027. [CoverMyMeds](https://www.covermymeds.health/) [CMS final rule](https://www.cms.gov/initiatives/burden-reduction/overview/interoperability/policies-regulations/cms-interoperability-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f)

#### 12. Clinical handoffs preserve facts but lose the prospective situation model

- **Actor / trigger:** A nurse or physician transfers patient responsibility at a shift or unit boundary.
- **Desired outcome:** The receiver understands trajectory, rationale, contingencies, outstanding tasks, and what may happen next.
- **Current workaround:** Verbal report, EHR notes, templates, and structured checklists.
- **Cost/harm:** A study summarized by AHRQ examined 498 closed malpractice claims, found communication failures in nearly half, and found failed handoffs in 47% of communication-error claims. This is a selected sample of severe claims, not hospital-wide prevalence. [AHRQ PSNet, 2022](https://psnet.ahrq.gov/issue/frequency-and-nature-communication-and-handoff-failures-medical-malpractice-claims) **[Q]**
- **Why tools fail:** Templates trade completeness for attention; the record is fragmented; latent concern such as “what I am watching for next” is poorly represented.
- **Contradiction:** People need a concise shared prospective situation model, but current systems force a static note beside a fragmented record.
- **Confidence:** High for the mechanism; medium for prevalence. AHRQ’s 2024 review says handoff failure is commonly involved in harm but often one component of a larger causal cascade. [AHRQ, 2024](https://effectivehealthcare.ahrq.gov/products/structured-handoff/protocol)
- **Saturation:** Generic clinical summary and handoff tooling is heavily served; an ordinary summarizer is not whitespace.

### Saturated spaces to deprioritize

Pain can be real while the obvious product shape is already crowded:

- Generic meeting, inbox, and project catch-up: [Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/copilot/microsoft-365-copilot-overview)
- Cloud incident summarization and triage: [PagerDuty](https://www.pagerduty.com/platform/incident-management/incident-response/)
- Security-questionnaire completion: [Conveyor](https://www.conveyor.com/landing-page/security-questionnaire-automation-software)
- Prior-authorization packet automation: [CoverMyMeds](https://www.covermymeds.health/)
- Construction drawing/version control: [Procore](https://www.procore.com/en-ca/project-management/drawings)
- Public-defense transcription/search: [JusticeText](https://www.justicetext.com/)
- Freight detention/demurrage audit and recovery: [Windward](https://windward.ai/solutions/demurrage-detention-automation/), [Unwaived](https://www.unwaived.com/), [AuditDray](https://auditdray.com/)
- Social-work case-note dictation: [Binti AI](https://binti.com/binti-ai/), [Magic Notes](https://www.carahsoft.com/magic-notes)

The clearest whitespace pattern across the strongest cards is not “help a worker write or summarize.” It is a recurring high-consequence event where responsibility crosses a boundary, evidence is fragmented or asymmetric, and existing systems record completion without proving that the intended real-world state was achieved.
